HTML entities are codes that makes possible to render special characters (for ex- the copyright symbol or emojis) on the webpage.
These special characters are not present on the keyboard which makes it harder for us to include them into the webpage without
the use of html entities.
Some commonly used html entities:
    &lt; - Represents the less-than sign <.
    &gt; - Represents the greater-than sign >.
    &amp; - Represents the ampersand &.
    &quot; - Represents the double quotation mark ".
    &copy; - Represents the copyright symbol Â©.
